THIS MATTER came before the Board of Medicine pursuant to
Section 120.57(2), Florida Statutes, on March 29, 1996, in Ft.
Lauderdale, Florida. At the hearing, Petitioner was represented by
Larry G. McPherson, Jr., Chief Medical Attorney. Respondent was
present at the hearing.

On February 26, 1996, Heafing Officer William Kendrick entered
an Order Closing File and Relinquishing Jurisdiction finding that
there was no disputed issue of fact. On February 28, 1996, the
Petitioner notified the Respondent by mail that this matter would
be presented at the March 29—31, 1996 meeting. On March 12, 1996,
the Petitioner notified Respondent by certified mail that this
matter would be heard on March 29, 1996.

Respondent was not present at the hearing. No material facts
were in dispute, therefore, an informal hearing was conducted
pursuant to Section 120.57(2), Florida Statutes. Upon

consideration of the Administrative Complaint, the Motion for Final

Order, the arguments of the Petitioner, and having been otherwise
fully advised, the Board of Medicine makes the following findings
and conclusions:

INDINGS 0 ACT

1. On June 26, 1995, the Agency for Health Care
Administration filed an Administrative Complaint against Clara
Diaz, M.D., seeking to take disciplinary action against her license
to practice medicine in the state of Florida.

2. The Board adopts as its findings of fact the allegations
set forth in the Administrative Complaint filed in this cause and
which is attached to this Final Order and incorporated herein by
reference.

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW

1. The Board has jurisdiction over the parties and subject
matter of this case pursuant to Section 120.57(2), Florida Statutes
and Chapter 458, Florida Statutes. ‘

2. Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, Respondent
violated Sections 458.331(1)(k) and (x), Florida Statutes and
having determined that violations exist, it is appropriate for the
Board to impose disciplinary action against the license of Clara

Diaz, M.D.
DISPOSITION
WHEREFORE, it is hereby ORDERED and ADJUDGED that Respondent
has violated Sections 458.331(1)(k) and (x), Florida Statutes, the
Respondent shall receive the following penalty:

1. Upon the filing of the Final Order in this Cause, the

Respondent’s license to practice medicine in the State of Florida
is hereby REVOKED.

2. The Respondent shall pay an administrative fine in the
amount of ($5,000) five thousand dollars to the Board of Medicine,
within thirty (30) days of the filing of the Final Order in this
cause, pursuant to Rule 59R-8.002, Florida Administrative Code.

This Final Order becomes effective upon its filing with the
Clerk of the Agency for Health Care Administration.

NOTICE

The Parties are hereby notified that they may appeal this
Final Order by filing one copy of a Notice of Appeal with the Clerk
of the Agency for Health Care Administration and by filing one copy
of a Notice of Appeal and the appropriate filing fee with the
District Court of Appeal within thirty (30) days of the date this
Final Order is filed.

ADMINISTRATIVE COMPLAINT

COMES NOW the Petitioner, Agency for Health Care
Administration, hereinafter referred to as "Petitioner," and files
this Administrative Complaint before the Board of Medicine against
Clara Diaz, M.D., hereinafter referred to as "Respondent," and
alleges:

1. Petitioner is the state agency charged with regulating the
practice of medicine pursuant to Section 20.42, Florida Statutes;
Chapter 455, Florida Statutes; and Chapter 458, Florida Statutes.

2. Respondent is and has been at all times material hereto a
licensed physician in the State of Florida, having been issued
license number ME 0028421. Respondent’s last known address is

10550 N.W. 77th Court, Suite 301—302, Hialeah Gardens, Fl. 33016.

3. Respondent is a non—board certified family practice
physician.
4. Respondent during the times material hereto was a

physician employed at the Total Family Treatment Clinic.

5. Patient A.R., a 63 year old female, never presented to
Respondent for medical services of any kind. Patient A.R. has never
met or heard of Respondent before this investiqatioh.

6. Respondent, while employed at the Total Family Treatment
Clinic, authorized the Neuro Vascular Institute to bill Medicare
for services rendered to Patient A.R. in the amount of $6,575.00.

7. Neuro Vascular Institute after receiving authorization
from Respondent, billed Medicare in the above stated amount, for a
non-invasive doppler study performed on or about February 23, 1993.

8. Patient A.R. never received a non-invasive doppler study
and she has never been to the Neuro Vascular Institute for any
medical services.

9. Respondent failed to keep a current address of her medical
practice on file with the Board of Medicine.

10 Section 458.319(3), Florida Statutes, states, that a
licensee must have on file with the department the address of his
primary place of practice within this state prior to engaging in
that practice. Prior to changing the address of his primary place
of practice, whether or not within this state, the licensee shall
notify the department of the address of his new primary place of
practice.

Count One

11. Petitioner realleges and incorporates paragraphs one (1)
through ten (10) as if fully set forth herein this Count One.

12. Respondent is guilty of violating any provision of this

chapter, a rule of the board or department, or a lawful order of

the board or department previously entered in a disciplinary
hearing or failing to comply with a lawfully issued subpoena of the
department, in that Respondent failed to notify the department of
the address of her new primary place of practice.

13. Based on the foregoing, Respondent violated Section
458.331(1)(x), Florida Statutes, and is guilty of violating any
provision of this chapter, a rule of the board of department, or a
lawful order of the board or department previously entered in a
disciplinary hearing or failing to comply with a lawfully issued
subpoena of the department.

Count Two

14. Petitioner realleges and incorporates paragraphs one (1)
through ten (10) and twelve (12) as if 'fully set forth herein this
Count Two.

15. Respondent is guilty of making deceptive, untrue, or
fraudulent representations in or related to the practice of
medicine or employing a trick or scheme in the practice of
medicine, in that Respondent authorized the Neuro Vascular
Institute to bill Medicare for approximately $6,575.00 for services
Respondent never performed for Patient A.R. '

16. Based on the foregoing, Respondent violated Section
458.331(1) (k) , Florida Statutes, and is guilty of making deceptive,
untrue, or fraudulent representations in or related to the practice

of medicine or employing a trick or scheme in the practice of

medicine.

Count Three

17. Petitioner realleges and incorporates paragraphs one (1)
through ten (10) twelve (12) and fifteen (15) as if fully set forth
herein this Count Three.

18. Respondent is guilty of exercising influence on the
patient or client in such a manner as to exploit the patient or
client for financial gain of the licensee or of a third party,
which shall include, but not be limited to, the promoting or
selling of services, goods, appliances, or drugs, in that
Respondent fraudulently authorized the Neuro Vascular Institute to
bill Medicare for services Respondent or the Neuro Vascular
Institute never performed on Patient A'RT

19. Based on the foregoing, Respondent violated Section
458.331(1)(n), Florida Statutes, and is guilty of exercising
influence on the patient or client in such a manner as to exploit
the patient or client for financial gain of the licensee or of a
third party, which shall include, but not be limited to, the

promoting or selling of services, goods, appliances, or drugs.

WHEREFORE, the Petitioner respectfully requests the Board of
Medicine enter an Order imposing one or more of the following
penalties: permanent revocation or suspension of the Respondent's
license, restriction of the Respondent's practice, imposition of an
administrative fine, issuance of a reprimand, placement of the
Respondent on probation, and/or any other relief that the Board

deems appropriate.
