FINAL ORDER

 

THIS CAUSE came before the Board of Medicine (Board)
pursuant to Section 120.57(2), Florida Statutes, on May 31, {991,

in Orlando, Florida, for consideration of the Administrative

cause. At the hearing, Petitioner was represented by Larry G.
McPherson, Jr., Acting Chief Medical Attorney; Respondent
appeared at the hearing with James Busch, Esquire. The facts are
not in dispute.

Upon consideration, it is ORDERED:

1. The allegations of fact set forth in the Administrative
Complaint, with the exception of paragraphs 11, 12, and 13, are
approved and adopted and incorporated herein by reference as the
findings of fact by the Board.

2. The conclusions of law alleged and set forth in the
Administrative Complaint are approved and adopted and
incorporated herein by reference as the conclusions of law by the

Board.

3. The violations set forth warrant disciplinary action by
the Board. THEREFORE,

IT IS HEREBY ORDERED AND ADJUDGED:

That the Respondent shall pay an administrative fine of
$1,000 with such fine to be paid within thirty (30) days of the
effective date of this Final Order, to the Executive Director of
the Board of Medicine, 1940 North Monroe Street, Tallahassee,
Florida 32399-0750. Furthermore, Respondent‘s license to
practice medicine in the State of Florida shall be suspended
until such time as he satisfactorily completes the SPEX
examination and otherwise complies with the requirements to
reactivate a medical license as set forth in Rule 21M—28, Floiida
Administrative Code. The Board also hereby reimposes the
original stipulation entered into by Respondent and Petitioner in
DPR Case No. 0073026. Such terms shall be for a period of four
(4) years.

This order takes effect upon filing with the Clerk of the
Department of Professional Regulation.

ADMINISTRATIVE‘COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, the Petitioner, Department of Professional
Regulation, hereinafter referred to as "Petitioner," and files this
Administrative Complaint before the Board of Medicine against Romeo
D. Acosta, M.D., hereinafter referred to as "Respondent," and
alleges:

1. Petitioner is the state agency charged with regulating the
practice of medicine pursuant to Section 20.30, Florida Statutes,
Chapter 455, Florida Statutes, and Chapter 458, Florida Statutes.

2. Respondent is and has been at all times material hereto
a licensed physician in the State of Florida, having been issued
license number ME 0038093. Respondent's last known address is 272
South University Drive, Plantation, Florida 33324.

3. On or about October 10, 1988, Respondent agreed to and
signed a Stipulation as set forth in DPR Case No. 0073026. A copy
of the Stipulation is attached hereto and incorporated by reference
as Exhibit A.

4. As a term of the Stipulation, Respondent's license to

practice medicine was placed on probation for a period of four (4)

5. As a condition of probation, Respondent was to notify the

6. As a condition of probation, Respondent's monitoring
physician was to submit reports on a quarterly basis, in affidavit
form. Respondent was responsible for ensuring that his monitoring
physician submitted the required reports.

7. As a condition of probation, Respondent was to submit

8. As a condition of probation, Respondent was to have

Respondent's compliance with the terms and conditions of his

Order against Respondent, approving and adopting the Stipulation.

during his probationary period, and submit to the Board affidavits
detailing the community service performed.
